Display Font — 3 directions
Aneesh Simha

A — Keep DM Serif Display
Warm, reliable, already loaded
Clean transitional serif. Already works with the palette. Add texture through text-shadow and letter-spacing rather than changing the face. Practical — no new font loading.

B — Cormorant Garamond
Lineage, elegance, carved stone
Based on Garamond — one of the oldest type families in the West. Light weight at large sizes feels inscribed, not printed. High contrast between thick and thin strokes gives it that letterpress quality. Free on Google Fonts.

C — Playfair Display
Bold presence, editorial weight
High-contrast transitional design inspired by Baskerville. More dramatic thick-thin variation. Feels like a magazine masthead — editorial authority. Could be too loud for "eloquent craftsman" energy.
